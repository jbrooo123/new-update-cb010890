Turtle Categories


Any reptile with a bony shell covering its body, including tortoises, is a turtle (member of the order Testudines). Despite the fact that many animals, including mammals and invertebrates, have developed shells, none have an architecture like that of turtles. There is a top (carapace) and a bottom (plastron) to the turtle's shell. Bony structures called the carapace and plastron typically connect to one another along either side of the body to form a hard skeletal box. The turtle keeps this box, which is made of bone and cartilage, all of its life. Since the shell is an essential component of the body, the turtle cannot leave it and it is not shed like some other reptiles' skin.There are about 356 different species of turtles that live on land, in freshwater and saltwater, on every continent but Antarctica. Five of the seven marine turtle species—the green turtle, olive ridley, hawksbill (Eretmochelys imbricata), loggerhead (Caretta caretta), and leatherback (Dermochelys coriacea)—can be found in Sri Lankan waters.
