Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery


Along the nations southern shore,there are 18 turtle hatcheries.One of the earliest turtle converation invitiatives in Sri Lanka is the Kosgoda turtle hatchery,which was established in 1981. It is well known for producing the five species of turtles found on the nations beaches. The kosgoda projects evision doing much more to protect the turtle life along the shore than only serving as a nursery. For locals and visitors from other countries to participate in and learn about the proccesses of turtle hatching, they conduct a variety of activities and volunteer programs.

Activities & Hatching Process Undertaken by Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

By raising hatching rates, Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery continues to pursue its steadfast objective of protecting sea turtle eggs. In addition to merely hatching, they also take care of fishing-related turtles that are frail, injured, or paralyzed. The volunteers patrol the shoreline at night in search of turtles that are about to lay eggs. The volunteers transport the eggs from the beach to the hatchery once the mother turtles have finished laying their eggs. The development and hatching of the eggs could take 45 to 60 days. Baby turtles are swiftly released back into the water after hatching, usually in the late evenings to lessen bird and crab predation.
